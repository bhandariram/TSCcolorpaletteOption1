Design Philosophy
Why These Colors?
Every color in this palette is intentionally tied to the Tamang cultural identity, the organisational goals, and the practical needs of a community-focused digital platform.

A Palette That Tells a Story

The Tamang Society of Canada serves a unique role — it bridges the ancient heritage of the Tamang people with the modern lives of its diaspora in Canada. The color palette must honour both worlds.

These colors are not arbitrary. They are drawn from the Tamang cultural landscape: the deep indigo of Himalayan twilight, the ceremonial red of traditional dress, the gold of Buddhist dharma wheels and temple ornaments, and the green of Nepal's terraced hillsides.

Together, they create a visual language that feels simultaneously familiar to the Tamang community and professional enough for a modern Canadian organisation.

01

Cultural Authenticity

Each color traces back to a tangible element of Tamang life — from temple architecture to mountain landscapes — ensuring the brand feels genuinely rooted.

02

Trust & Professionalism

Navy and crimson together convey institutional authority, important for an organisation managing memberships, payments, and official certificates.

03

Warmth & Accessibility

Gold and green soften the formality, making the platform feel welcoming to all ages — from elders to youth — and inviting community participation.

04

Digital Clarity

Every pairing passes WCAG AA contrast standards, and the ramp system provides consistent shading for hover states, disabled elements, and UI feedback.

Cultural Rationale
Why Each Color Suits the Organisation
Every color maps to a specific aspect of the Tamang Society's identity, purpose, and operational needs.

Himalayan Navy — Trust & Heritage

The deep navy reflects the towering Himalayan ranges that define Tamang homeland. As the primary colour, it communicates institutional stability — essential for an organisation handling memberships, official certificates, and community funds. It says: "We are established, trustworthy, and rooted."

#1E3A5F

Tamang Crimson — Action & Ceremony

Red holds deep significance in Tamang culture — it is the colour of tika, of festivals like Lhosar, and of celebration. As the secondary colour, it drives user action: "Apply Now," "Join Today," "Pay Membership." The energy of crimson transforms passive visitors into active community members.

#B22234

Dharma Gold — Spirituality & Identity

Gold is drawn directly from the society's logo — the dharma wheel / mandala motif central to Tamang Buddhist heritage. Used as the accent colour, it highlights what matters most: membership badges, featured events, hover states, and the logo itself. It bridges the sacred and the modern.

#D4A843

Sagarmatha Green — Growth & Approval

Named after Mount Sagarmatha (Everest), this green represents Nepal's natural landscape and the society's growth trajectory. In the WebApp, it is the colour of positive feedback: approved applications, successful payments, downloaded certificates, and community milestones reached.

#4A7C59

Cloud Mist Neutrals — Clarity & Breathing Room

A community WebApp is used by people of all ages and varying digital literacy. The neutral system — from Snow (#FAFBFD) to Charcoal (#1E2328) — ensures the interface remains uncluttered and readable. Alternating mist-coloured sections create visual rhythm without overwhelming. The warm-toned grays (rather than cold blue-grays) subtly complement the gold accent, keeping the overall feeling welcoming rather than corporate. This is critical for a society that serves elders, families, and youth equally.

Application Guide
Where Each Color Lives
Quick reference for the development team (S2 & S3) to apply colours consistently across the WebApp.

Header, Footer & Hero

Navy-600 background with white text. Top bar uses Navy-700 for subtle depth separation.

Primary CTA Buttons

Crimson gradient (500→300) with white text. Hover shifts to 600→400. Active state drops shadow.

Badges & Highlights

Gold-400 for featured badges, hover borders on cards, and star ratings. Text on gold uses Gold-800.

Success & Approval States

Green-400 for checkmarks, approved badges, payment confirmation toasts, and certificate download icons.

Section Backgrounds

Alternating Snow/Mist sections. Cards use white (#FFF) with Border (#E2E6EC) stroke on Mist backgrounds.

Error & Warning States

Crimson-50 background with Crimson-700 text for form validation errors and required field alerts.

Info & Tooltip Backgrounds

Navy-50 with Navy-700 text for informational banners, tooltips, and membership status pills.

Typography System

Headings use Charcoal (#1E2328). Body text uses Body (#5A6A7D). Captions use Muted (#8A9AB5).
